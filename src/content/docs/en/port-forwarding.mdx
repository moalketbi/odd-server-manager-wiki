If friends outside your home can't connect to your server, this page is for you.

## What's happening

Your computer hosts the server, but your router stands between the internet and your computer. By default, the router blocks unknown incoming traffic. Port forwarding tells the router: *"when traffic for ARK arrives, send it to my computer."*

## Which ports to forward

| Port | Protocol | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **7777** | UDP | Game traffic (players connecting) |
| **7778** | UDP | Game traffic (raw) |
| **27015** | UDP | Steam server browser (so the server appears in lists) |

You forward all three to **your computer's local IP** (looks like `192.168.x.x`).

## Find your computer's local IP

Open Command Prompt and run:

<CopyBlock>ipconfig</CopyBlock>

Look for the line **IPv4 Address** under your active connection. That's the address you give the router.

## How to actually do it on your router

This is where instructions stop being universal — every router brand has a different settings page.

<Tip label="What to do">
  1. Find your router model (sticker on the back, or label on the bottom).<br/>
  2. Search the web for **`<your router model> port forwarding`** — there are step-by-step guides for nearly every model.<br/>
  3. Forward ports **7777, 7778, 27015** (all UDP) to your computer's IPv4 address from above.
</Tip>

<Tip>Some ISPs use **CGNAT** — meaning even with port forwarding, your home isn't directly reachable. If port forwarding doesn't work after correct setup, contact your ISP and ask for a public IP, or use a tunneling service like Playit.gg.</Tip>
